NAVSIM-DT — How to Use
ProximaED Naval Digital Twin Platform v4 · Educational Research Guide
Quick Start
1. Select Scenario
Click a scenario card. Bay of Bengal Patrol is pre-loaded with INCOIS baseline ocean data.
2. Set Controls
Ballast → 100%, Throttle → 60%, Dive Planes → −12° (nose-down to dive).
3. Press START
All 8 physics panels, sonar scope, depth chart, and AI Instructor activate. Or press AUTO DEMO.
Controls
Throttle 0–100%
F_thrust = (thr/100) × 250,000 N. Drag decelerates at 0%.
Dive Planes −35° to +35°
Negative = nose-down = dive. Velocity-dependent effect.
Ballast 0–100%
0% = surface (1,500 t). 100% = submerged (1,810 t). Controls buoyancy.
Sonar: Passive
Listen only. Ping every 10 s. SNR = SL − TL − (NL − DI).
Sonar: Active
Emit ping every 5 s. Better detection. Uses 2TL (two-way loss) + TS.
Sonar: Silent
No emissions. Contact probability frozen. Best for stealth.
Sonar Display (PPI Scope)
Full 360° circle
North = up. Bearing 0° = top. 90° = right. 145° (Contact α) = lower-right. 220° (Contact β) = lower-left.
Green sweep line
Sonar scan arc. Rotates continuously. Blips appear where contacts are detected.
Contact α (green)
Always visible as faint ghost. Brightens with each confirmed ping detection. Grows as range closes.
Contact β (amber)
Appears when you dive below 100 m. Bearing 220°.
Thermocline band
Horizontal blue line. Crossing it activates the ACOUSTIC SHADOW ZONE — passive sonar from above cannot hear you.
